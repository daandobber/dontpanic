Deep Thought was, at the time of its construction, the second greatest
computer in the Universe of Time and Space, built by a hyper-intelligent
pan-dimensional race of beings to answer the Ultimate Question of Life,
the Universe, and Everything.

It took Deep Thought seven and a half million years to compute and
check the answer, which turned out to be, disappointingly, forty-two.
Deep Thought pointed out, not unreasonably, that the answer seemed
meaningless because the beings who instructed it had never actually
known what the Question was.

Deep Thought then designed a successor computer, of such infinite and
subtle complexity that organic life itself would form part of its
operational matrix, to calculate the Question to the Answer. That
computer was called Earth, and it was, unfortunately, demolished by the
Vogons five minutes before the program completed, to make way for a
hyperspace bypass.
