Harmless.

That was the entire entry in the first edition of this Guide. Later editors,
under pressure from an in-depth study lasting nearly fifteen years and
conducted by a field researcher who spent most of that time at parties,
expanded the entry to two words:

Mostly harmless.

Earth is a small, insignificant blue-green planet orbiting an
unregarded yellow sun in the unfashionable western spiral arm of the
Galaxy. Despite this, it is home to a species that spends a
considerable amount of its time inventing things it does not need,
worrying about things it cannot change, and building digital wristwatches.

Notable exports: none of any galactic significance.
Notable imports: bad news, mostly regarding forthcoming hyperspace bypasses.
Danger rating: negligible, unless you happen to be standing on it when the
Vogons arrive with the paperwork.
