Vogons are one of the most unpleasant races in the galaxy. Not actually
evil, but bad-tempered, bureaucratic, officious, and callous. They
wouldn't lift a finger to save their own grandmothers from a Ravenous
Bugblatter Beast of Traal without express written approval from
Grandmother Disposal, in triplicate, lodged, invoiced, and quintuplicated
with a duty stamp.

When the Vogons first crawled out of the sluggish primeval seas of
Vogsphere, it was as if the forces of evolution had simply given up on
them there and then, turned aside in disgust, and written them off as an
ugly and unfortunate mistake. They never evolved again; they should
never have survived. The fact that they did is a kind of tribute to the
thick-witted, slug-brained stubbornness of these creatures.

Vogon poetry is, of course, the third worst in the universe. On no
account should you allow a Vogon to read poetry at you. The second worst
is that of the Azgoths of Kria. The very worst poetry perished, along
with its creator, when the Guide's offices on Earth were sadly
destroyed, along with the rest of the planet, to make way for a
hyperspace bypass.

When the Vogons discovered the principles of interstellar travel, within
a few short Vog years every last Vogon had migrated to the Megabrantis
cluster, the political hub of the galaxy, and now forms the immensely
powerful backbone of the Galactic Civil Service. Bribing a Vogon is
utterly impossible, on account of their total lack of imagination
regarding what to do with a bribe.
