The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy is an indispensable companion to
all those who are keen to see the marvels of the Universe for less than
thirty Altairian dollars a day.

It is a wholly remarkable book. It has been compiled and recompiled many
times over many years by many hands, of widely differing wisdom and
temperament, and though it falls short of the definitive perfection
that is required of it, it remains one of the more remarkable pieces
of freelance journalism to have come out of the Ursa Minor
publishing corporation.

Though it has many omissions, and contains much that is apocryphal, or
at least wildly inaccurate, it scores over the more pedestrian
Encyclopedia Galactica in two important respects. First, it is
slightly cheaper. Second, it has the words DON'T PANIC inscribed in
large friendly letters on its cover.

This offline edition, adapted for handheld data terminals, contains no
guarantee of accuracy whatsoever, and the editors accept no
responsibility for consequences arising from its use, misuse, or being
dropped in a swimming pool.
