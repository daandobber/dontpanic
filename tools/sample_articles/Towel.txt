A towel, the Guide notes, is about the most massively useful thing an
interstellar hitchhiker can carry, and this for quite a number of
reasons.

You can wrap it around you for warmth as you bound across the cold
moons of Jaglan Beta. You can lie on it on the brilliant marble-sanded
beaches of Santraginus V, inhaling the heady sea vapours. You can sleep
under it beneath the stars, which shine so redly on the desert world of
Kakrafoon. You can use it to sail a mini raft down the slow heavy river
Moth. You can wet it for use in hand-to-hand combat. You can wrap it
round your head to avoid the gaze of the Ravenous Bugblatter Beast of
Traal (a mindboggingly stupid animal which assumes that if you can't
see it, it can't see you). You can even, in extremities, use it to flag
down a passing spacecraft, or wave it in the air in mock, or genuine,
distress.

More importantly, a towel has immense psychological value. Any
non-hitchhiker who sees a hitchhiker in possession of a towel will
automatically assume that they are also in possession of a toothbrush,
face flannel, soap, tin of biscuits, flask, compass, map, ball of
string, gnat spray, wet-weather gear, space suit, and so on. Furthermore
the non-hitchhiker will then automatically assume that the hitchhiker
is also carrying a mind-bogglingly capable and resourceful individual
who is clearly not to be messed with.

Hence, a hitchhiker with a towel is a person to be reckoned with.
