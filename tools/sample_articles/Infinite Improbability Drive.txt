The Infinite Improbability Drive is a wonderful new method of crossing
vast interstellar distances in a mere nothingth of a second, without all
that tedious mucking about in hyperspace.

It was discovered by generating a strong Brownian Motion producer (say,
a nice hot cup of tea) and using it to power a Finite Improbability
Generator, which was connected to the atomic vector plotter already
existing in all matter transference beams, then feeding into this the
whole infinite improbability of a vessel's precise position and
non-position in space and time.

The drive works by making a spaceship briefly become every particle in
the universe simultaneously, before reassembling itself in a random
(and often mildly improbable) location. Side effects can include, but
are not limited to: temporary transformation into unlikely objects,
spontaneous knitting, and the sudden appearance of bowls of petunias
and irate sperm whales.

The odds against a working drive being discovered by chance were
calculated at exactly two to the power of infinity to one against, a
figure which a bright young student on Earth then proved was not,
actually, all that big a number.
